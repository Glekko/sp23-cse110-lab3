Interactive Notes

These are interactive notes. You can click on the summary to expand or collapse the content.

Understanding the differences between <i> and <em> and <b> and <strong> was a bit difficult for the group at the beginning.

However, we learned how to distinguish between their uses through these links:

  • Strong
  • Em

Apparently, things used to be much clearer in HTML4, because <i> and <b> used to just have explicit meanings. This meant that they just meant text being bold and italic, while <em> and <strong> had semantic meanings. That meant that there was an underlying difference in what using these tags meant. In HTML 5, however, all the tags are semantic, apparently trying to clarify what exactly the difference between the text semantics. Long story short, <strong> and <em> are used when you need to emphasize or say something strongly. Italics would be restricted to things that wouldn't affect speech, but maybe you'd want to point out in text, like book titles. For <em>, think levi-OH-saw, not levio-saw. For <strong>, think of things you'd want people to notice the importance of. Warnings, for example, should be in <strong> tags, as you want people to be aware of the contents.
